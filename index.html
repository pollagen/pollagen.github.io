About the project

Poll‑A‑Gen — a century of genome diversity in UK insect pollinators. Scientists at the Natural History Museum, funded by the Natural Environment Research Council (NERC), are measuring how the genetic diversity of important UK insect pollinators — bees, wasps and hoverflies — has changed over the last 100 years.

By combining DNA from freshly collected specimens with DNA recovered from century-old museum collections, the project is building a historical baseline of genome diversity and assessing how diversity and selection in these species have shifted over a century. It is currently the largest study of museum insect population genomics in the world, and the team hopes its methods will inspire historical genome baselines for other species and countries.

The results will help partners design better policies and strategies for insect conservation and support future research on crop pollination. The project is a collaboration between the Natural History Museum, Imperial College London, the University of East Anglia, the University of Plymouth and the Bumblebee Conservation Trust — and it relies on entomologists across the UK collecting and submitting specimens.
